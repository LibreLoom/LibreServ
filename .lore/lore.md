# The PIGEONVERSE

## Part 1: Prequal

### Chapter 1: Before the Clipboard

#### Scene 1: The Dark Times

Before the Clipboard, there was naught but pigeons.

Things were, but were not together. Everywhere, but nowhere, only one of each thing.

Nothing, except the pigeon.

The pigeon saw all, flew above all (bar the sun), but yet, he was not happy. The pigeon lived alone, no Clipboard, no paper, no pen.

Time and time went by, individualized bird reduced to mere instincts. From crumb to crumb, snack to snack, he simply was.

---

#### Scene 2: The First Sign of Order

One day, as the sunless sun rose above all, the pigeon noticed something unusual

The few crumbs, when not eaten formed piles.
Small piles. Uneven piles. But piles nonetheless, breaking the unbreakable pattern of individuality.

The pigeon did not know why this mattered; only that it did.
Not an intern. Just observant.

Nearby, a small mouse watched.
Not an intern either. Yet. Just observant.

---

#### Scene 3: The Discovery

The Clipboard was found leaning against nothing in particular.

It was not labeled.
It was not shiny.
It had no paper.

Yet the pigeon picked it up.

Nothing happened.

Nothing. But something.

There was something off, something changed. Change changed from the only thing absent from the world, to the only thing not changing.

But the change did not create chaos. It did not create order.

It revealed it.

---

### Chapter 2: The Change of Static Chaos

#### Scene 1: The First Act of Order

The pigeon did not start with rules.

It started with a pile.
A pile on a Clipboard.

1. Crumbs
2. Clipboards
3. Change

The list was not neat.
But it existed.

The mouse came closer.

This felt important.

---

#### Scene 2: Resistance

Not everyone appreaciated the list.

Not only the mouse was watching.

A seagull appeared.

The seagull laughed at the Clipboard.
Refusing to bend as low as a crumb.
She took. A snack.

The pigeon had now:

1. Crumbs
2. Clipboards
3. Change
4. Snacks

---

#### Scene 3: The Mouse

The mouse showed themselves.

"Why write it down?"

The pigeon paused.

He looked at the list.

So it would be constant.
No change

"But, Oh pigeon, change... Change is the only constant."

#### Scene 4: The Exchange

The pigeon looked.

First at the Clipboard
At the pile.
At the list that was already outdated.

Crumbs were eaten.
Snacks moved.
The world in constant change.

And yet.
The Clipboard remained.

#### Scene 5: The Anchor

The next day, the pigeon returned to the Clipboard.
And the next.
And the next.
Each time, nothing was the same.
But the Clipboard was where he had left it.

The pigeon placed things there.
Not to organize them, not to eat them.

But to watch them. To see the change.

A crumb left overnight.
A snack returned later.
A list crossed out, then uncrossed.

The Clipboard did not stop change.

It showed it.

---

### Chapter 2: The First Memory

#### Scene 1: The Problem with Change

On a day, the seagull returned.

She took a snack without looking.
Laughed again.

"Your flat rock does nothing," said she.

The pigeon said nothing.

Instead, he noticed something frightening.

He could not remember where the snack had been before.

---

#### Scene 2: The Observation

That night, the pigeon was with the Clipboard.

Change was constant.
But information was not.

If things were to be understood, they would have to be kept.

Not as piles.
Not as objects.

As marks.

---

#### Scene 3: The First looking

The pigeon found a pen.

None remember where it was from.
Only that it fit the Clipboard perfectly.

He flipped the Cliboard to the next page and wrote a single word.

Snacks

The world did not change.
The word did not change.

But finally, the pigeon could.

---

### Chapter 3: The Change of Time

Time changed.

The world changed.
So did the words.

And the pigeon evolved.

The pigeon found more ways, none as perminant as the Clipboard, yet different.

The mouse, once not an intern, now an intern.
The seagull, loud, logical, never wrong, a senior engineer.
A turtle, HR.

The world was changing.

So was the pigeon.

And one day, he wrote on the Clipboard.

> ### Chapter 1: Before the Clipboard

Stopped. Retracted his pen. Extended it again.

> #### Scene 1: The Dark Times

And then. He wrote.

> Before the Clipboard, there was naught but pigeons.

## Part 2: Closing Note

The Clipboard had always been more than a surface.

It was a promise.

That things could be remembered.
That order could be gentle.
That change could be observed without fear.

**Pronouns:** he/him  
**Role:** Keeper of Order  
Observes patterns, writes things down, believes order should be gentle.

---

### The Mouse Intern  
**Pronouns:** they/them  
**Role:** Learner  
Asks questions, learns systems, represents what comes next.

---

### The Seagull  
**Pronouns:** she/her  
**Role:** Disruption  
Acts on impulse, tests boundaries, reminds order why it exists.

---

### The Raccoon Accountant  
**Pronouns:** he/him  
**Role:** Numbers  
Counts resources, questions totals, finds value in everything.

---

### The Turtle from HR  
**Pronouns:** she/her  
**Role:** Process  
Moves slowly, de-escalates, ensures fairness.

---

### The Owl Auditor  
**Pronouns:** he/him  
**Role:** Accountability  
Appears rarely, demands documentation, leaves quietly.

---

### Snacks  
**Pronouns:** it/they  
**Role:** Morale  
Shared resource, symbol of trust and fairness.
